title: "Skills: Consistent Filing Instructions for Your Brain"

---

A skill is a filing instruction: it tells whoever is filing an item how to read it, where to put what it contains, and how to record where it came from. Skills are what keep filing consistent, so a meeting filed by your agent ends up looking exactly like a meeting filed by Cortex or by you.

Think of skills as the standing instructions pinned above the librarian's desk. "When a meeting transcript comes in: pull out the attendees, make a page for the meeting dated correctly, note any decisions, and cite the transcript." Everyone who files follows the same instructions, every time.

## The built-in skills

Cortex ships with a set of ready-made skills that cover the most common kinds of items you will send to a brain. You do not need to configure them; they are available the moment you start filing.

| Skill | Used for | What it does |
|---|---|---|
| **Ingest** | General items | Reads an item, works out what kind of thing it is, and files it sensibly. The default when nothing more specific applies. |
| **Ingest meeting** | Meeting transcripts | Attendees become people pages, the meeting becomes a properly dated page, and any decisions are recorded. |
| **Ingest document** | Uploaded documents | Pulls out the substance and files it as readable pages rather than a raw dump of the original. |
| **Ingest conversation** | Agent conversations | Captures what was decided or worked out, not the whole back-and-forth. |
| **Promote** | Personal-to-shared knowledge | Moves specific knowledge from your personal brain into a shared brain, deliberately and cleanly. |

## How your agent uses skills

When you connect an agent to a brain, these skills are available to it as ready-made instructions. You can direct the agent explicitly ("file this using the meeting skill") or simply ask it to file something and let it pick the right one. Either way, the result follows the same conventions as everything else in the brain: proper structure, links between related pages, and a citation back to the source.

<Tip>
If you are not sure which skill to specify, leave it to the agent. It will read the item, identify the type, and reach for the appropriate skill on its own.
</Tip>

## Consistent by design

The point of skills is that it does not matter who does the filing. Whether Cortex files an item automatically, your agent files it on request, or you file it by hand, the same instructions apply. Your brain stays tidy and predictable however things get into it, and recall stays reliable as it grows.

<Note>
Skills apply at filing time. If you want already-filed pages to reflect a new convention, you will need to re-file or edit those pages manually.
</Note>
