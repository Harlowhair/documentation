Recall is the payoff. Everything you capture and file exists so that later, when you or an agent needs it, it comes back. Cortex finds things by meaning, not just by matching words, which makes searching feel less like querying a database and more like asking someone who was there.

## Search understands what you mean

Ask a brain "what did we decide about pricing?" and it does not just hunt for the word "pricing". It understands the intent behind the question and returns the pages that are actually about that decision, even if they phrase things differently. This is what makes recall feel like asking a knowledgeable colleague rather than running a keyword search.

You can search a brain yourself from the app, and your connected agents search through their [connection](/connect/how-connections-work). Same brain, same results, whichever way you ask.

<Tip>
Write searches as plain questions rather than keyword strings. "What were the outcomes of the Q2 planning session?" will return better results than "Q2 planning outcomes".
</Tip>

## What makes recall good

The quality of what comes back depends on how well things were filed in the first place. This is the whole reason [structure](/filing/pages-and-structure) matters.

| What good filing provides | Why it helps recall |
|---|---|
| **Citations** | Answers arrive with their sources, so you can trust them and check them. |
| **Links between pages** | Finding one page often surfaces the related ones around it, giving broader context. |
| **Clean, readable pages** | Search reads tidy write-ups rather than raw transcripts, so it finds the substance rather than the noise. |

A brain that has been filed well recalls well. That is why Cortex puts effort into the filing step: it is an investment in every search that follows.

## Recall through your agent

The most powerful way to use recall is through an agent connected to your brain. Instead of searching yourself and reading through results, you ask the agent a question and it does the searching, reads the relevant pages, and answers you directly, with citations.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Connect your agent to a brain">
    Give your agent access to one or more brains through a Connection. The agent can then search those brains on your behalf.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ask a plain question">
    Ask the agent something naturally. It searches the brain by meaning, reads the pages it finds, and pulls together an answer.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check the citations">
    The agent's answer will include citations pointing back to the pages it used. Follow them to verify or read further.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Over time, as your brain fills up, the agent becomes more useful because it has more to draw on. Recall compounds.

<Info>
Your agent and you always search the same brain. There is no separate index for agents; they use the same pages you see in the app.
</Info>
