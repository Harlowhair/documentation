The review queue is where items wait when Cortex has a suggestion but will not commit it. That includes things it was not confident enough to file automatically, as well as everything it looked at when a brain is set to Propose mode. Think of it as your desk for the "please check this" pile: nothing leaves it without your say-so.

## What you see for each item

Every item in the queue gives you three things to work with before you decide:

- **The suggested filing.** Where Cortex thinks the item should go and what kind of page it should become.
- **The reasoning.** A short explanation of why it made that suggestion, so you are not left guessing at its logic.
- **The item itself.** The original content, so you can check the suggestion against the actual source before committing.

## Your three choices

<Steps>
  <Step title="Approve">
    Accept the suggestion as-is. Cortex creates the page and marks the item as filed. Use this when the suggestion looks right and you have nothing to change.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Adjust">
    Change the destination, the page type, or other details, then file. Use this when Cortex's instinct was close but not quite right. You get the benefit of its suggestion without being locked to it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reject">
    Decide the item does not belong in the brain at all and discard it. The item is removed from the queue and nothing is filed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Nothing leaves the review queue without your decision. A brain will never fill up with filings you did not agree to.
</Note>

## When something will not file

Occasionally an item resists filing altogether. It might be malformed, or Cortex might keep failing to make sense of it after several attempts. Rather than retrying forever, Cortex gives up after a few tries and parks the item in the review queue so you can handle it by hand.

A stuck item is always surfaced; it is never silently dropped. You will see it in the queue with a note explaining that automatic filing did not succeed, and you can decide what to do with it from there.

<Tip>
If you see a stuck item in the queue, check whether the source file is complete and readable. Re-uploading a clean version and rejecting the stuck one is usually the quickest fix.
</Tip>
