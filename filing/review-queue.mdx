---
title: "The review queue"
description: "Deciding on the items Cortex parked for you"
---

The review queue is where items wait when Cortex has a suggestion but will not commit it: things it was not confident enough to file automatically, or everything it looked at when a brain is set to Suggest. It is your desk for the "please check this" pile.

## What you see

Each item in the queue comes with:

- **The suggested filing:** where Cortex thinks it should go, and as what.
- **The reasoning:** a short explanation of why, so you are not guessing at its logic.
- **The item itself:** the original content, so you can check the suggestion against the source.

## Your three choices

- **Approve:** accept the suggestion as-is. The page is created and the item is filed.
- **Adjust:** change the destination or details, then file. Use this when the instinct was close but not quite right.
- **Reject:** decide it does not belong, and discard it.

Nothing leaves the queue without your say-so, so a brain never fills up with filings you did not agree to.

## When something will not file

Occasionally an item resists filing: it is malformed, or Cortex keeps failing to make sense of it. Rather than retrying forever, Cortex gives up after a few attempts and parks the item in the review queue for you to handle by hand. A stuck item is always surfaced, never silently dropped.

## Try it

1. Set a brain to Suggest and drop a few items in its inbox.
2. Open the review queue and read the suggestion and reasoning for each.
3. Approve one, adjust another, and reject a third, then check the brain to see the results.
