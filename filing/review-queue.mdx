---
title: "The review queue"
description: "Deciding on the items Cortex parked for you"
---

The review queue is where items wait when Cortex has done the thinking but will not commit without you: things it was not confident enough to file automatically, and everything it looked at when a brain is set to Suggest. It is your desk for the "please check this" pile.

## Most items arrive with the work already done

When Cortex parks an item, it usually attaches a **proposal**: the actual pages it would write, drafted in full, together with where they would go, how confident it is, and why. You are not being asked "where should this go?" - you are being shown finished pages and asked "shall I file these?"

For each drafted page you can see whether it is a **new page** or an **update** to an existing one, and for updates, the current page side by side with the proposed replacement.

## Your choices

- **Accept and file:** the drafted pages are written to the brain, the item is archived, and the link between source and pages is recorded. One click.
- **Edit, then accept:** every draft is editable in place. Change what you like and accept; your version is what gets written.
- **Reject:** the drafts are discarded and the item returns to the queue, ready for another pass or for manual filing.
- **File manually:** skip the proposal entirely. Open the item, choose a folder, and write the page yourself; the editor starts pre-filled with the extracted text. Saving writes the page and archives the item in one step.

Nothing leaves the queue without your say-so, so a brain never fills up with filings you did not agree to.

## You will not have to remember to check

Waiting items are surfaced wherever you happen to be:

- **In the app:** your brain cards show an "awaiting review" badge that links straight to the inbox.
- **In your agent:** connected agents can see the same counts, and will mention parked items and offer to walk you through them - you can review and accept entirely in conversation.
- **By email:** if items have been waiting more than a day, brain admins get a short morning digest listing which brains need attention, with links.

## When something will not file

Occasionally an item resists filing: it is malformed, or Cortex keeps failing to make sense of it. Rather than retrying forever, Cortex gives up after a few attempts and parks the item for you to handle by hand. A stuck item is always surfaced, never silently dropped.

## Try it

1. Set a brain to Suggest and drop a few items in its inbox.
2. Open the review queue and read a proposal: the summary, the confidence, the drafted pages.
3. Accept one as-is, edit another before accepting, and reject a third, then check the brain to see the results.
