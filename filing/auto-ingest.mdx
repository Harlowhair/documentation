---
title: "Auto Filing: Control How Much Cortex Files for You"
sidebarTitle: "Auto Filing"

---

Items in your Inbox can be filed by you, by your agent, or by Cortex itself. How much Cortex does on its own is a per-brain setting called its filing mode. You can dial it from "do nothing automatically" all the way up to "file confidently and only pause when unsure". The right setting depends on how much you trust the brain's content to be filed without a second pair of eyes.

## The three filing modes

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Off" icon="circle-pause">
    Cortex files nothing on its own. Items wait in the Inbox until you or your agent file them. Full manual control, nothing happens without your action.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Propose" icon="lightbulb">
    Cortex looks at each item and suggests a filing, but does not commit it. The suggestion and the reasoning both wait for you in the review queue.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Automatic" icon="bolt">
    Cortex files items it is confident about and parks anything uncertain in the review queue. The steady, hands-off mode for a brain you trust.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

In every mode, uncertainty always comes back to you. Even on Automatic, Cortex never forces a filing it is unsure of. It parks the item in the [review queue](/filing/review-queue) instead and waits for your decision.

## What each brain starts on

Cortex picks a sensible default for each brain type, though the Owner can change the mode at any time in the brain's settings.

- **Your personal brain** starts on **Automatic**. It is yours alone, so confident filing there is safe. This keeps your private memory tidy without requiring any effort from you.
- **New shared brains** start on **Propose**. A shared brain is other people's space too, so Cortex suggests rather than commits until you decide how much to trust it.

<Info>
Only a brain's Owner can change its filing mode. Members and Contributors see the results of whatever mode the Owner has set.
</Info>

## Automatic filing has a daily allowance

Automatic filing does not run at full speed without limit. Cortex works through the Inbox steadily, but it cannot burn through an unlimited amount of work in a single burst. If there is more than a day's worth of items waiting, it simply continues the next day.

This keeps automatic filing predictable and prevents any surprise from a sudden flood of items arriving at once.

<Warning>
If you connect a data source with a large backlog, Cortex will work through it over several days rather than all at once. Check the Inbox and review queue over the following days to see progress.
</Warning>
