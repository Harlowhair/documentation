When something is filed, the result is a page. A page is one piece of knowledge written as readable text: a person, a meeting, a decision, a topic. Pages are what you and your agents actually read and recall from. Raw source material goes in; a clean, structured page comes out.

## What a good page has

Filing is not a simple copy-and-paste. Each page is written so that it holds up on its own and connects to the rest of the brain around it.

- **A clear title and readable text.** Not a raw dump of the source, but a tidy write-up a person would be happy to read. Your agents read pages the same way you do, so clarity helps both of you.
- **A place in the structure.** Pages live in folders, organised the way the brain's template lays out, so related things sit together and the brain stays navigable as it grows.
- **Links to related pages.** A meeting page links to the people who attended; a project page links to its decisions. Following links is often faster than searching, and it is how agents find context they were not specifically asked for.
- **Citations.** At the bottom of every page is a note of where its information came from and when. This is what lets you, and your agents, trust what a page says: you can always trace it back.

## Nothing is lost to an edit

Every page keeps its full version history. When a page changes, whether you edit it or an agent files an update into it, the previous version is kept. You can look back at what a page said before and see how your knowledge evolved over time.

<Note>
Editing is safe. You can never quietly overwrite something and lose it. If you want to see what a page said before a change, open its version history and compare.
</Note>

## Why structure makes recall better

A well-structured brain is easier to recall from, for you and for your agents. The effort that goes into filing is really an investment in what comes back later.

- **Citations** mean answers arrive with their sources, so you can trust them and check them.
- **Links** mean related knowledge is one hop away, not buried in a separate search.
- **Sensible folders** mean things are where you would look for them.
- **Consistent skills** mean the structure stays predictable as more people and agents contribute to a brain.

<Info>
The quality of recall depends directly on the quality of filing. Pages that are well-structured, clearly written, and properly cited return the best results when you or an agent searches.
</Info>
