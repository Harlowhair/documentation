Cortex is a place to keep your thinking, so it is entirely fair to ask what happens to it. This page gives you a plain, honest picture of where your data lives, how it is protected, and who can see what. No small print.

## Where your data lives

Your Brains, Pages, and files are stored on managed cloud infrastructure. Content is encrypted at rest. The databases that power Cortex are not exposed to the public internet: they sit on a private network that only the application can reach. Your knowledge is held securely, not sitting in the open.

## Who can see your content

| Who | What they can see |
|---|---|
| **You** | Everything in your own Brains, both personal and any shared Brains you belong to |
| **Your team members** | Only the shared Brains you have given them access to, at the role you granted, and nothing beyond that |
| **No one** | Your personal Brain: not teammates, not workspace administrators, not Cortex staff |

There is no exception to the personal Brain rule. It has no members, cannot be shared, and that is built into how Cortex works rather than being a setting that could be changed or overridden.

## The personal Brain guarantee

The reason your [personal Brain](/brains/your-personal-brain) can be a safe default for everything you capture is that its privacy is structural. There is no configuration under which someone else gains access to it.

Anything that lands in your personal Brain (an agent conversation, a quick upload, an incoming meeting note) is visible only to you unless you deliberately [promote](/brains/promoting) it somewhere else. You never have to worry about capturing something sensitive in your personal Brain by mistake.

<Info>
  Promoting content copies it from your personal Brain to a shared one. Your original stays in your personal Brain, private to you, until you choose to remove it.
</Info>

## Connections and access

When you connect an agent to a Brain, that Connection gets access to exactly one Brain at the access level you approved at the time of connection, and nothing more. You can see every active Connection on a Brain's Connect page. You can revoke any Connection instantly, and it stops working immediately.

Nothing is connected that you did not approve. Nothing stays connected that you revoke.

<Note>
  An agent Connection can never exceed your own access level on a Brain. If you hold a Read role, any agent you connect to that Brain also holds Read. See [Roles & Permissions](/teams/roles-and-permissions) for detail.
</Note>

## Deleting your data

- **Pages:** you can delete any Page you have permission to manage. The version history goes with it.
- **Shared Brains:** the Brain's Owner can delete the Brain and all its contents.
- **Your account:** your personal Brain is part of your account. Deleting your account removes your personal data.

If you need help with a specific export or deletion request, get in touch with the Cortex team directly.

<Warning>
  Deletion is permanent. Cortex does not retain copies of deleted Brains or Pages after removal. If you are unsure, archive a Brain rather than deleting it.
</Warning>
