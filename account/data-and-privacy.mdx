Cortex is a place to keep your thinking, so it is fair to ask what happens to it. Here is the honest picture.

## Where your data lives

Your brains, pages and files are stored on managed cloud infrastructure. Content is encrypted at rest, and the databases behind Cortex are not exposed to the public internet: they sit on a private network that only the application can reach. In short, your knowledge is held securely, not sitting in the open.

## Who can see your content

- **You** can see everything in your own brains.
- **Your team members** can see the shared brains you have given them access to, at the role you granted, and nothing more.
- **No one** can see your personal brain. Not teammates, not workspace administrators. It has no members and cannot be shared. This is built into how Cortex works, not a setting that could be changed.

## The personal-brain guarantee, restated

The reason [your personal brain](/brains/your-personal-brain) can be the safe default for everything you capture is that its privacy is structural. There is no configuration in which someone else gains access to it. Anything that lands there, an agent conversation, a quick upload, an incoming meeting, is visible only to you unless you deliberately [promote](/brains/promoting) it elsewhere.

## Connections and access

When you connect an agent, it gets a key to one brain at the access level you approved, and no more. You can see every connection on a brain's Connect page and revoke any of them instantly. Nothing is connected that you did not approve, and nothing stays connected that you revoke. [More on connections.](/connect/how-connections-work)

## Deleting your data

You can delete pages, and their version history goes with them. You can delete shared brains you own. Your personal brain is part of your account; deleting your account removes your data. If you need help with a specific deletion, get in touch.
