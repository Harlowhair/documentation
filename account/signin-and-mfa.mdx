Getting into Cortex is straightforward, and keeping your account secure is equally simple. You have two ways to sign in and two options for adding a second layer of protection. This page walks through both, and shows you how to set up two-factor authentication step by step.

## Signing in

Cortex offers two sign-in methods:

- **Email and password.** Register with your email address, confirm it using the link in the email Cortex sends you, and sign in with your credentials from then on.
- **Google.** Sign in with your Google account in one click. No separate Cortex password to remember.

You can use whichever suits you. If you originally registered with your email address and later want the convenience of signing in with Google, you can do so: use the same email address and Cortex will recognise your account.

## Two-factor authentication (MFA)

Two-factor authentication adds a second step to signing in beyond your password. Even if someone else obtains your password, they still cannot access your account without the second factor.

Cortex offers two methods:

- **Authenticator app.** Scan a QR code with an app such as Google Authenticator or 1Password. Each time you sign in, you enter the six-digit code the app generates. This is the most secure option.
- **Email codes.** Cortex sends a code to your registered email address each time you sign in. You enter that code to complete the sign-in.

### Setting up two-factor authentication

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open your security settings">
    Go to your account security settings and choose to set up two-factor authentication. You will be prompted to pick a method.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose your method and confirm it">
    For an **authenticator app**: scan the QR code shown on screen with your app, then type the six-digit code the app displays to confirm the link is working.

    For **email codes**: select email as your method. Cortex will send a test code to confirm your address is reachable.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save your recovery codes">
    When you enable an authenticator app, Cortex gives you a set of recovery codes. Save these somewhere secure: a password manager is ideal. If you ever lose your device, recovery codes are how you get back into your account.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
  Recovery codes are shown only once. If you lose them and lose your authenticator device at the same time, regaining access to your account will require contacting support. Save them before leaving the setup screen.
</Warning>

## Changing your details

Your name, password, and other profile information are all in your account settings. You can update them at any time.

<Note>
  Changing your password does not sign out your connected agents. Agent Connections are managed separately on each Brain's Connect page and continue working until you revoke them explicitly.
</Note>
