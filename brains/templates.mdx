When you create a brain, one of the first things you do is pick a template. A template is the brain's starting structure: the folders it is organised into, the kinds of Pages it expects, such as people, meetings, projects, and decisions, and the guidance the filing Skills follow when they decide where something belongs. You are not locked in to what the template gives you, but starting with the right one means the librarian has a filing system ready on day one rather than having to invent one under pressure.

## Why structure matters

Consistent filing depends on consistent rules. When a meeting transcript arrives in an Inbox, the librarian should not have to guess where it goes: the template already says meetings are filed by date, that attendees each get a person Page, and that any decisions made are recorded in a place the team knows to look. Without that guidance, even the most capable librarian will make different choices on different days, and the brain becomes harder to search over time.

A good template does not constrain what you put in. It means what you put in ends up somewhere sensible.

<Info>
Templates affect the initial folder structure and the filing guidance given to Skills. They do not restrict what types of content you can add, and you can always create new folders and Page types as your needs grow.
</Info>

## The starter templates

| Template | Built for | What it includes |
|---|---|---|
| **Personal** | Your own private memory | Folders for people you know, projects you are on, notes, and ideas. Used by your Personal brain automatically. |
| **Company** | A shared team knowledge base | Folders for people, clients, meetings, decisions, and processes. |
| **Strategy** | Plans, goals, and reasoning | Structure for bets, goals, and the thinking behind them, with history you can revisit as circumstances change. |
| **Client** | One client relationship | Folders for contacts, meetings, commitments, and ongoing context for a single client. |
| **Blank** | No opinions | An empty brain with no pre-set folders. Build your own structure, or let it emerge from what you actually put in. |

## Picking the right one

Pick the closest fit, not the perfect one. No template will match your situation exactly on day one, and that is fine. A template is a starting point: you can add Pages and folders at any time, rename what does not suit you, and let the structure grow around what you actually put in the brain.

If you are not sure, **Company** is the most broadly useful choice for team brains, and **Blank** is the right call if you already have a structure in mind and do not want a template's opinions in the way.

<Tip>
Before you create a new shared brain, spend a moment browsing the template gallery. Seeing the folder structures laid out side by side makes it much easier to spot which one fits your use case.
</Tip>

## Try it

1. Create a new brain and browse the template gallery before committing to one.
2. Pick **Company**, then look at the empty folder structure it created.
3. Drop in a meeting transcript and notice where the librarian files it.
