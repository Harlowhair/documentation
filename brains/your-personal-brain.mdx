Every Cortex account has exactly one personal brain. Yours was created the moment you joined, it is called Personal, and three things are permanently true about it regardless of any plan, setting, or workspace configuration. Understanding these three facts is the fastest way to understand why the Personal brain works the way it does.

## The three permanent truths

**It cannot be shared.** There is no share button to find, no setting to flip, and no role to grant. The Personal brain is not "private until you change it": it is private, full stop. If you are looking for a way to give someone access, it does not exist.

**It cannot be deleted.** The Personal brain is a structural part of your account. It exists as long as your account exists, and there is no workflow to remove it.

**No one can be invited into it.** Teammates in your workspace can share other brains with you, but nobody, including workspace admins, can see inside your Personal brain or receive any kind of access to it.

<Note>
These restrictions are not bugs or missing features. They are deliberate product decisions. The rest of this page explains why.
</Note>

## Why so strict?

Your Personal brain is the **default destination** for everything you capture. When your agent saves a conversation, when you drop a file into an Inbox without stopping to think, when a connected tool pushes something in overnight, it all lands in your Personal brain unless you deliberately pointed it somewhere else.

A default destination only works as a safe landing zone if it is completely, unconditionally private. You should never have to stop and ask "wait, who else will see this?" before capturing a half-formed thought, a sensitive client note, or a rambling conversation with your agent. The answer is always: nobody. That is only trustworthy if it can never quietly become untrue, which is why the privacy is built into the structure of the brain rather than controlled by a switch.

The strictness is the feature. Capture freely. Decide what to share later, deliberately.

<Tip>
Think of your Personal brain as a private notebook that also has a brilliant librarian. Write whatever you like; the librarian will file it neatly and find it for you later. Nobody else reads the notebook.
</Tip>

## Getting things out of your Personal brain

Keeping everything private forever is rarely the goal. Knowledge often starts private and earns its way to the team: you capture meeting notes privately, and later the decision buried in those notes belongs in the team brain where everyone can find it.

The route for that is [promoting](/brains/promoting). Promoting is a deliberate copy of specific knowledge from your Personal brain into a shared brain you have Write access to. You choose exactly what gets copied, where it goes, and when. It is the only outbound path from a Personal brain, and it never happens automatically.

<Info>
When you promote a Page, your personal original stays exactly where it is. Promoting is a copy, not a move.
</Info>

## Try it

1. Open your Personal brain and look at its settings panel: notice what is absent. There are no members, no sharing controls, and no delete option.
2. Ask your connected agent to *"save a note that I prefer morning meetings"* and watch the item arrive in your Personal brain's Inbox.
3. Check the filed Page afterwards: private, cited, and yours alone.
