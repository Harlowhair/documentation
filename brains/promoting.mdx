Knowledge usually starts private and earns its way to the team. You capture a client call into your Personal brain, and most of what is in there stays there: your impressions, your half-formed thoughts, the tangents that went nowhere. But the decision made on that call belongs in the team brain, where everyone can find it. Promoting is how it gets there.

Promoting is a deliberate copy of specific knowledge from your Personal brain into a shared brain you have Write access to. It is the **only** path out of a Personal brain, and it never happens automatically. Nothing is promoted unless you say so.

## How promoting works

You invoke the **Promote** Skill, either directly or by asking your agent, point it at what should be shared, and name the destination brain. The Skill then does three things in sequence.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Read the source">
    The Skill reads the relevant material in your Personal brain: a specific Page, a section, or a filed item you have identified.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write a clean Page in the shared brain">
    The Skill writes a new Page in the destination brain (or updates an existing one), phrased for a team audience. This is not a verbatim copy. The Skill distils what is relevant and writes it in a way that makes sense to someone who was not part of the original capture.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Cite the promotion as the source">
    The new Page in the shared brain records when and where the knowledge came from. Your private notes are not exposed. The citation shows that the content was promoted and when, without revealing anything from your Personal brain.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Your personal original stays exactly where it was. Promoting is a copy, not a move.

<Note>
A Connection to a shared brain is required for the Promote Skill to write there. If you do not yet have a Connection to your destination brain, set one up first from the Connections section of that brain.
</Note>

## A worked example

**Tuesday, 2:00pm.** Your agent captures the transcript of a supplier call into your Personal brain. The transcript contains pricing gossip, your gut feelings about the account manager, a few dead-end tangents, and one real decision: the team is switching to annual billing.

**Tuesday, 2:05pm.** You tell your agent: *"Promote the annual billing decision to the Team Handbook brain."* The agent uses its Team Handbook Connection to write one tidy Page in that brain: the decision, the date it was made, and the reasoning behind it. Your gut feelings and the pricing gossip stay in your Personal brain. Nobody else sees them.

That is the whole model: capture everything without worrying, then share the parts that matter, on purpose, when you are ready.

<Tip>
You do not have to promote one item at a time. You can ask your agent to review a set of notes and promote everything that meets a specific condition, for example: "Promote any decisions from this week's client calls to the Client brain."
</Tip>

## What stays private

Promoting is selective by design. You choose what to promote, and the Skill writes a Page that contains only what you pointed it at. The following always remain in your Personal brain and are never visible to anyone else:

- The full original capture (transcript, note, or file)
- Anything you did not explicitly include in the promotion instruction
- Your personal impressions, reactions, or annotations

<Info>
The citation on the shared Page confirms that a promotion happened and records the date. It does not link back to or describe anything in your Personal brain.
</Info>

## Try it

1. Ask your agent to save a note with two facts in it: one personal, one relevant to a team brain you have access to.
2. Ask it to promote just the team-relevant fact to that shared brain.
3. Open both brains and compare what each one contains.
