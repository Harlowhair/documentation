---
title: "Promoting to a shared brain"
description: "The one deliberate path from private to shared"
---

Knowledge usually starts private and earns its way to the team. You capture a client call into your personal brain, and most of it stays there: your impressions, your half-thoughts, the tangents. But the decision made on that call belongs in the team brain, where everyone can find it.

Promoting is how it gets there: a deliberate copy of specific knowledge from your personal brain into a shared brain you have Write access to. It is the **only** path out of a personal brain, and it never happens on its own.

## How it works

You (or your agent, on your instruction) invoke the **promote** skill, point it at what should be shared, and name the destination brain. The skill:

1. Reads the source material in your personal brain.
2. Writes a clean page (or updates an existing one) in the shared brain, phrased for a team audience rather than copied verbatim.
3. Cites the promotion itself as the source, so the shared page records when and where it came from, without exposing your private notes.

Your personal original stays exactly where it was, untouched.

## A worked example

Tuesday, 2pm: your agent captures the transcript of a supplier call into your Personal brain. It contains pricing gossip, your gut feelings about the account manager, and one real decision: the team is switching to annual billing.

Tuesday, 2:05pm: you tell your agent, *"Promote the annual billing decision to the Team Handbook brain."* The agent uses its Team Handbook connection to write one tidy page: the decision, the date, the reasoning. Your gut feelings stay private.

That is the whole model: capture everything without worrying, share the parts that matter, on purpose.

## Try it

1. Ask your agent to save a note with two facts in it, one personal, one team-relevant.
2. Ask it to promote just the team-relevant fact to a shared brain.
3. Open both brains and compare what each one now contains.
