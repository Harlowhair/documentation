title: "Shared brains"
description: "Where teams build knowledge together, deliberately"
---

A shared brain is a memory your team builds together: the company knowledge base, a client project, a product area. Unlike your personal brain, shared brains are created on purpose, shared on purpose, and written to on purpose.

## Nothing arrives by accident

The most important thing about shared brains is what does *not* happen. Nothing flows into them automatically. There is no setting that routes your captures to a shared brain by default, and an agent connected to your personal brain cannot write to a shared one, because its connection physically reaches only the brain it was approved for.

For something to appear in a shared brain, someone with Write access must deliberately put it there: by working in that brain's inbox, through an agent connection approved for that specific brain, or by [promoting](/brains/promoting) something from their personal brain.

## Members and roles

People are added to a shared brain with one of four roles:

| Role | In plain words |
|---|---|
| **Read** | Can browse pages, search, and read the inbox. Cannot change anything. |
| **Write** | Everything Read can do, plus add to the inbox, file items, and create or edit pages. |
| **Admin** | Everything Write can do, plus manage members, connections and brain settings. |
| **Owner** | Everything Admin can do, plus delete the brain. Every brain has an owner. |

Agent connections carry a role too: when you connect an agent to a shared brain, the approval screen shows the access level the connection will have, and a connection can never do more than the person who approved it.

## Creating one

From **Brains**, choose to create a new brain, give it a name, pick a [template](/brains/templates) that matches what it is for, and invite members. It starts empty; its inbox works exactly like your personal one.

## Try it

1. Create a shared brain called "Team Handbook" using the Company template.
2. Invite a teammate with the Write role.
3. Drop a document into its inbox and watch it become the brain's first page.
