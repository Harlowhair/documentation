Cortex is built on five nouns. Understand these and the rest of the product explains itself: every feature you encounter is simply one of these ideas in action. This page defines each concept clearly and links to the deeper reference if you want to go further.

## Brain

A Brain is one self-contained memory. It has its own Pages, its own Inbox, its own search index, and its own Connections. Your **Personal Brain** is created with your account, is private to you permanently, and can never be shared or deleted. You can also create or join **Shared Brains** with your team, each of which operates independently with its own privacy controls.

## Page

A Page is one filed piece of knowledge: readable text about a person, a meeting, a decision, a project, or any other topic. Each Page has a title, a folder location within the brain, links to related Pages, citations back to the source material, and a full version history so nothing is ever lost to an edit.

## Inbox

Every Brain has an Inbox, and everything arrives there first: file uploads, meeting transcripts, agent conversations. Nothing writes straight into the brain; items wait in the inbox until they are filed, whether by Cortex automatically, by your connected agent, or by you manually. Think of the inbox as the tray sitting on top of the filing cabinet.

## Skill

A Skill is a filing instruction. It tells Cortex how to read a particular kind of item (a meeting transcript, a contract, a research note), where to put what it contains, and how to record where it came from. When your connected agents file things, they follow Skills too, which keeps filing consistent no matter who, or what, does it.

## Connection

A Connection links one AI agent to exactly one Brain. You approve each Connection on a screen that names the brain and the access level being granted, and you can revoke any single Connection at any time without affecting the others. This is the key-to-one-cabinet rule: an agent can only reach the specific brain you have authorised it to use.

## The privacy promise

<Note>
  Your Personal Brain is the default destination for everything you capture and can never be shared with anyone. Shared Brains only ever receive what you deliberately send them through a Connection you have approved. Privacy in Cortex is a matter of architecture, not a setting you might accidentally turn off.
</Note>
