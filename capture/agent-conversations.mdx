The conversations you have with AI agents are full of knowledge: decisions, reasoning, things you worked out together. Cortex can capture those conversations into your brain automatically, so your agent's memory builds itself as you work.

When a conversation ends, a short summary of it is posted to your inbox, where it is filed like anything else. You do not copy anything by hand; it just accumulates.

## The default destination

There is a special inbox address that always routes content into your default brain, which for most people is their personal brain. This is what makes automatic capture safe: everything your agents record about your work arrives somewhere only you can see. You decide later what, if anything, belongs in a shared brain.

## Setting it up in Claude Code

Claude Code can run a command when a session ends. You point that command at your default inbox using a [service credential](/connect/service-credentials) so it can post without needing a browser.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a service credential">
    In your personal brain's service credentials, create a read-and-write credential. Label it something like `claude-code-capture` so you can identify it later, then copy its `ck_` key.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a session hook">
    In your Claude Code settings, add a hook that runs when a session ends and posts the transcript to your default inbox:

    ```bash
    curl -s https://api.oncortex.ai/api/v1/inbox \
      -H "Authorization: Bearer ck_your_key_here" \
      -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
      -d "{\"filename\": \"session-$(date +%Y%m%d-%H%M).md\", \"content\": $(jq -Rs . < transcript.md)}"
    ```

    The exact wiring depends on your setup, but the shape is always the same: when the session ends, send its summary to `/api/v1/inbox` with your credential.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Let it run">
    From this point on, each session's summary lands in your personal inbox and is filed into your brain. Start your next session by asking *"what did we work on last time?"* and your agent can tell you.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Other agents

Any agent or tool that can run a command or call a web address when it finishes can do the same thing: post the conversation to `https://api.oncortex.ai/api/v1/inbox` with a service credential. The default-inbox address means you never have to name a specific brain; it always goes to your private one.

## Keeping it tidy

Captured conversations are filed by the [conversation skill](/filing/skills), which pulls out the substance rather than dumping raw transcripts into the brain. If two sessions capture the same material, duplicate handling keeps your brain from filling up with repeats.
