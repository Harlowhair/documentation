---
title: "Capturing agent conversations"
description: "Turn your AI conversations into memory automatically"
---

The conversations you have with your AI agents are full of knowledge: decisions, reasoning, things you worked out together. Cortex can capture those conversations into your brain automatically, so your agent's memory builds itself as you work.

The idea is simple. When a conversation ends, a short summary of it is posted to your inbox, where it gets filed like anything else. You do not copy anything by hand; it just accumulates.

## The default destination

There is a special inbox address that always lands in **your default brain**: for you, that is your personal brain. This is what makes automatic capture safe. Everything your agents record about your work arrives somewhere only you can see, and you decide later what, if anything, belongs in a shared brain.

## Setting it up in Claude Code

Claude Code can run a command when a session ends. You point that command at your default inbox, using a [service credential](/connect/service-credentials) so it can post without a browser.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create a service credential">
    In your personal brain's service credentials, create a read-and-write credential labelled something like "claude-code-capture" and copy its `ck_` key.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add a session hook">
    In your Claude Code settings, add a hook that runs when a session ends and posts the transcript to your default inbox:

    ```bash
    curl -s https://api.oncortex.ai/api/v1/inbox \
      -H "Authorization: Bearer ck_your_key_here" \
      -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
      -d "{\"filename\": \"session-$(date +%Y%m%d-%H%M).md\", \"content\": $(jq -Rs . < transcript.md)}"
    ```

    The exact wiring depends on your setup, but the shape is always the same: when the session ends, send its summary to `/api/v1/inbox` with your credential.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Let it run">
    From now on, each session's summary lands in your personal inbox and is filed into your brain. Ask your next session *"what did we work on last time?"* and it can tell you.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Other agents

Any agent or tool that can run a command or call a web address when it finishes can do the same thing: post the conversation to `https://api.oncortex.ai/api/v1/inbox` with a service credential. The default-inbox address means you never have to name a brain; it always goes to your private one.

## Keeping it tidy

Captured conversations are filed by the [conversation skill](/filing/skills), which pulls out the substance and files it, rather than dumping raw transcripts. If two sessions capture the same thing, [duplicate handling](/capture/the-inbox#duplicates-take-care-of-themselves) keeps your brain from filling up with repeats.
