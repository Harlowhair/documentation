If you use [Granola](https://granola.ai) to take meeting notes, you can connect it to a Cortex brain so your transcripts arrive and are filed automatically. You finish a meeting, and a little while later it is in your brain. No exporting, no copy-pasting.

## How it works

You add Granola as a **source** on a brain. From that point on, Cortex checks Granola periodically and brings in new meeting notes as they appear. It files each one using the meeting skill: attendees become people, the meeting becomes a dated page, and decisions are recorded where the brain keeps them.

Because it runs on a schedule, there is nothing to keep open and nothing to trigger manually. New meetings show up in the brain's inbox on their own and are filed from there like any other inbox item.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the brain's sources settings">
    Go to the brain you want your meetings to land in (most people start with their personal brain) and open its **Sources** settings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Granola as a source">
    Add a new source of type Granola, give it a label you will recognise, and provide your Granola access token so Cortex can read your notes on your behalf.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Let it run, or trigger a check now">
    Cortex will start checking for new meetings on its schedule. You can also trigger an immediate check to pull in your recent meetings straight away.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
Your Granola access token is stored encrypted and is never displayed after you save it. If you need to rotate it, create a new source and delete the old one.
</Info>

## Where meetings land

Send your meetings to whichever brain makes sense. For most people that is their personal brain, keeping notes private by default. You can then promote the parts that belong to the wider team when the time is right. If a brain is dedicated to a project or client, pointing Granola straight at it may be the cleaner choice.

## Duplicates and re-runs

Cortex tracks which meetings it has already brought in. Running a check twice does not create duplicate pages. It only ever files meetings it has not seen before.
