---
title: "Meetings with Granola"
description: "Have your meeting notes arrive on their own"
---

If you use [Granola](https://granola.ai) to take meeting notes, you can connect it to a Cortex brain so your meeting transcripts arrive and get filed automatically. No exporting, no copy-pasting: you finish a meeting, and a little while later it is in your brain.

## How it works

You add Granola as a **source** on a brain. From then on, Cortex checks Granola periodically and brings in new meeting notes as they appear, filing each one using the meeting skill: attendees become people, the meeting itself becomes a dated page, and decisions are recorded where the brain keeps them.

Because it runs on a schedule, there is nothing to keep open and nothing to trigger. New meetings show up in the brain's inbox on their own and are filed from there like anything else.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the brain's sources">
    Go to the brain you want your meetings to land in (often your personal brain) and open its **Sources** (ingest sources) settings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Granola">
    Add a new source of type Granola, give it a label, and provide your Granola access token so Cortex can read your notes on your behalf. The token is stored encrypted and never shown again.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Let it run, or run it now">
    Cortex will start checking for new meetings on its schedule. You can also trigger a check immediately to pull in your recent meetings straight away.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Where meetings land

Send your meetings to whichever brain makes sense. For most people that is their personal brain, keeping meeting notes private by default; you can then [promote](/brains/promoting) the parts that belong to the team. If a brain is dedicated to a project or client, pointing Granola straight at it can be the right call.

## Duplicates and re-runs

Cortex tracks which meetings it has already brought in, so running a check twice does not create duplicate pages. It only ever files meetings it has not seen before.

## Try it

1. Add Granola as a source on your personal brain.
2. Trigger a check and watch your recent meetings appear in the inbox.
3. Open a filed meeting page and see the attendees linked as people.
