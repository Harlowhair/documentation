title: "Roles and permissions"
description: "Who can do what, in plain words"
---

Access in Cortex is granted per brain. Being in a workspace does not give you access to its shared brains automatically; you are added to each brain with a role that says what you can do there.

## Brain roles

| Role | Can do |
|---|---|
| **Read** | Browse pages, search, and read the inbox. Cannot change anything. |
| **Write** | Everything Read can do, plus add to the inbox, file items, and create or edit pages. |
| **Admin** | Everything Write can do, plus manage the brain's members, connections and settings. |
| **Owner** | Everything Admin can do, plus change the brain's filing mode and delete the brain. Every brain has an Owner. |

## The questions people actually ask

**Can a Reader's agent write to the brain?** No. An agent connection can never do more than the person who made it. A Reader connecting an agent grants read-only access.

**Can a Writer delete pages?** A Writer can create and edit pages, and edits keep full [version history](/filing/pages-and-structure#nothing-is-lost-to-an-edit) so nothing is truly lost. Managing the brain itself, including its members and settings, is an Admin and Owner responsibility.

**Who can change how much gets filed automatically?** The brain's Owner sets its [filing mode](/filing/auto-ingest).

**Can anyone see my personal brain?** No one, ever, regardless of their role anywhere. [Personal brains](/brains/your-personal-brain) are outside this system entirely: they have no members and cannot be shared.

## Connections carry a role too

When you connect an agent to a brain, the connection is granted a role as well, shown on the approval screen. A connection can never exceed your own access, and you can revoke any single connection without affecting your membership or your other agents. [More on connections.](/connect/how-connections-work)
