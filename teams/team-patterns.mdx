Here are some common shapes that work well. Treat them as starting points.

## A team knowledge base

Create one shared brain, "Team Handbook" or similar, using the [Company template](/brains/templates). Add the team as members: most people as Write, a couple as Admin. Everyone files what they learn into it, or has their agent do so, and everyone can recall from it.

Because filing follows [skills](/filing/skills), the handbook stays consistent no matter who contributes. Start it on Propose so the team stays in control of what lands, and move it to Automatic once you trust it.

## A client or project brain

Create a shared brain per client or project. Point relevant [meetings](/capture/meetings-granola) straight at it, add the people working on that account as members, and keep everything about that relationship in one place: contacts, decisions, commitments, history. When the project ends, the brain is a complete, searchable record.

## Meetings into a shared brain

One person connects [Granola](/capture/meetings-granola) to a shared project brain, and the whole team's view of that project's meetings builds up in one place. Others can promote their own relevant meeting notes in from their personal brains as needed.

## Private first, shared deliberately

The pattern underneath all of these: people capture freely into their personal brains, and [promote](/brains/promoting) the parts that belong to the team. Nobody has to self-censor while capturing, and the shared brains only ever contain what someone chose to put there.
