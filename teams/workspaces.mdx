---

Your account lives in a workspace. On your own, the workspace is just you. When you work with a team, you share one workspace, and within it you build shared brains together while each keeping your own private personal brain.

<Info>
Inviting people to a workspace is a team feature and depends on your plan.
</Info>

## What a workspace holds

- **Shared brains** that members build together, each with its own membership and roles.
- **Every member's personal brain**, which stays private to that member. Being in a shared workspace never exposes your personal brain to anyone, not even a workspace administrator.

The workspace is the boundary of collaboration: people in it can be given access to shared brains; people outside it cannot.

## Inviting someone

Invite a person by email from your workspace's team settings. They receive an invite, join with their own login (or create one), and become a member. From then on you can add them to shared brains with whatever role fits.

## What a new member sees

A person you invite sees the shared brains you give them access to, and nothing else. They do not see your personal brain, your other private content, or shared brains they have not been added to. Access is always granted deliberately, brain by brain.

## Try it

1. Open your workspace's team settings and invite a colleague by email.
2. Once they join, add them to a shared brain as a Reader.
3. Confirm they can see that brain, and cannot see your personal one.
