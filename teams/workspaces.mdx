Your account lives in a workspace. On your own, the workspace is simply you. When you work with a team, you share one workspace and build shared Brains together, while each person keeps their own private personal Brain. Think of the workspace as the outer walls of the filing cabinet: everything inside belongs to the people who have a key.

<Info>
  Inviting people to a workspace is a team feature. Availability depends on your plan.
</Info>

## What a workspace holds

A workspace contains two kinds of thing:

| What | Who can see it |
|---|---|
| **Shared Brains** | Members you explicitly add to each Brain, at the role you grant them |
| **Personal Brains** | Only the individual member who owns that Brain, and no one else, ever |

Shared Brains are collaborative spaces that members build together. Each one has its own membership list and roles, so you control access Brain by Brain rather than all at once.

Every member's personal Brain stays entirely private to that member. Being part of a shared workspace never exposes a personal Brain to anyone, including workspace administrators. That privacy is structural: it is not a setting that can be toggled.

## The workspace as a collaboration boundary

The workspace defines who can be given access to your shared Brains. People inside your workspace can be added to shared Brains with appropriate roles. People outside your workspace cannot access any Brain you own, no matter what.

## Inviting someone to your workspace

Invite a person by email from your workspace's Team Settings. They receive an invitation, join with their own login or create one, and become a member. Once they are a member, you can add them to any shared Brain with whatever role fits their responsibilities.

<Tip>
  Start new members as Readers on a shared Brain so they can explore without changing anything. Promote them to Write once you are ready for them to contribute.
</Tip>

## What a new member sees

A person you invite sees only the shared Brains you explicitly give them access to. They do not see:

- Your personal Brain
- Other members' personal Brains
- Shared Brains they have not been added to
- Any private content that has not been shared with them

Access is always granted deliberately, Brain by Brain. Nothing becomes visible by accident.
