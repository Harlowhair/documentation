These questions come up most often when people start using Cortex. If something you are wondering about is not covered here, get in touch and we will add it.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Why can't I share my personal brain?">
    Your personal brain is the default destination for everything you capture, and that is only safe if it can never be seen by anyone else. If sharing were possible, you would have to think twice before capturing anything sensitive. Making it permanently private means you never have to.

    To share something from it, [promote](/brains/promoting) that specific page or collection to a shared brain. You stay in full control of what moves across.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why does each brain need its own connection?">
    So that privacy is guaranteed by structure, not by an agent's judgement. Each connection's key physically reaches one brain and only one brain. An agent can never accidentally write your private notes into a shared brain, because the key for your personal brain cannot write anywhere else.

    [More on how connections work.](/connect/how-connections-work)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="If I connect many brains, will my agent get slow or confused?">
    Connect the brains an agent actually needs for the work in front of it (usually your personal brain plus one or two project brains) and you will not notice any overhead. An agent might occasionally search the wrong brain and find nothing, which you can simply correct, but a wrong choice can never leak private content.

    [More on using multiple brains.](/connect/multiple-brains)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What file types can I upload?">
    Cortex accepts Markdown, plain text, PDF, Word (`.docx`), and PowerPoint (`.pptx`) files, up to 50 MB each. Cortex extracts the text from each file automatically, so the content becomes searchable straight away.

    [More on uploading files.](/capture/uploading-files)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Will Cortex file things without asking me?">
    Only in the mode you choose. Each brain has a filing mode:

    - **Off:** nothing is filed automatically.
    - **Propose:** Cortex suggests where to file each item and waits for your approval.
    - **Automatic:** Cortex files confidently and parks anything it is unsure about for your review.

    Your personal brain starts on Automatic. New shared brains start on Propose. In every mode, anything Cortex is unsure about waits for you before it moves.

    [More on filing modes.](/filing/auto-ingest)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Does automatic filing have a limit?">
    Yes. Automatic filing has a daily allowance so it works through your inbox steadily rather than in an uncontrolled burst. If there is more than a day's worth of items to process, Cortex continues working through them the following day.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens to an edited page?">
    Nothing is lost. Every page keeps its full version history, so you can always look back at what it said before any edit was made.

    [More on pages and structure.](/filing/pages-and-structure)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I get my data out?">
    Your pages are readable text and yours to keep. You can delete individual pages and any brains you own. Deleting your account removes your data from Cortex. If you need a specific export or deletion, get in touch and we will help.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How does search find things if I don't use the exact words?">
    Search understands meaning, not just keywords. A plain-language question finds the pages that are genuinely about it even when they are worded differently to how you phrased your query. You do not need to remember the exact phrase something was saved under.

    [More on search and recall.](/filing/search-and-recall)
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
