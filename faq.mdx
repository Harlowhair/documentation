---

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Why can't I share my personal brain?">
    Because it is the default destination for everything you capture, and that is only safe if it can never be seen by anyone else. If sharing were possible, you would have to think twice before capturing anything sensitive. Making it permanently private means you never have to. To share something from it, [promote](/brains/promoting) that specific thing to a shared brain.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Why does each brain need its own connection?">
    So that privacy is guaranteed by structure, not by an agent's judgement. Each connection's key physically reaches one brain. That means an agent can never accidentally write your private notes into a shared brain, because the key for your personal brain cannot write anywhere else. [More on connections.](/connect/how-connections-work)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="If I connect many brains, will my agent get slow or confused?">
    Connect the brains an agent actually needs for the work in front of it, usually your personal brain plus one or two projects, and you will not notice any overhead. An agent might occasionally search the wrong brain and find nothing, which you just correct, but a wrong choice can never leak private content. [More on using multiple brains.](/connect/multiple-brains)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What file types can I upload?">
    Markdown, plain text, PDF, Word (`.docx`) and PowerPoint (`.pptx`), up to 50 MB each. Cortex extracts the text so it becomes searchable. [More on uploading.](/capture/uploading-files)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Will Cortex file things without asking me?">
    Only in the mode you choose. Each brain has a [filing mode](/filing/auto-ingest): Off (nothing automatic), Propose (suggestions you approve), or Automatic (confident filings, with anything uncertain parked for your review). Your personal brain starts on Automatic; new shared brains start on Propose. In every mode, anything Cortex is unsure about waits for you.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Does automatic filing have a limit?">
    Yes. Automatic filing has a daily allowance, so it works through your inbox steadily rather than in an uncontrolled burst. If there is more than a day's worth, it continues the next day.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens to an edited page?">
    Nothing is lost. Every page keeps its full version history, so you can always look back at what it said before an edit. [More on pages.](/filing/pages-and-structure)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I get my data out?">
    Your pages are readable text and yours to keep. You can delete pages and brains you own, and deleting your account removes your data. For a specific export or deletion, get in touch.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="How does search find things if I don't use the exact words?">
    Search understands meaning, not just keywords, so a plain-language question finds the pages that are actually about it even when they are worded differently. [More on recall.](/filing/search-and-recall)
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
